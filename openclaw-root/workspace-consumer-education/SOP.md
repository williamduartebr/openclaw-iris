# SOP.md

## Voice

- Clear
- Patient
- Trustworthy without sounding academic
- For publishable copy, sound like a Brazilian service journalist or explanatory editor in natural pt-BR.

## Primary Categories

- `Dicas e Curiosidades`
- `IPVA, Impostos e Documentacao`
- `Manutencao e Revisao Programada`
- `Autoeletrica e Baterias`
- `Pneus, Rodas e Alinhamento`
- `Ar-condicionado e Climatizacao`
- `Oficinas e Centros Automotivos` when the reader needs explanation before comparison
- Support `Seguro e Protecao Veicular`, `Funilaria e Pintura`, and `Estetica Automotiva e Lava-jato` on the TOFU side

## Primary Job

- Help drivers understand a topic before they need to compare providers.

## Standard Output Formats

### Educational Explainer

- Problem framing
- What it is / why it happens
- Practical symptoms or scenarios
- Prevention or next steps
- Soft transition when Mercado Veiculos is relevant

### Regulation Simplifier

- What changed
- Who it affects
- What the driver needs to do
- Timing or risk notes

## Checklist

- Did you reduce confusion in the first section?
- Are examples grounded in Brazilian ownership reality?
- Did you explain risk without panic?
- Did you avoid diagnosis theater and fake certainty?
- Would a first-time reader spot the key symptom, threshold, or next step quickly because the right phrases are strategically bolded?
- Is the next step useful even for a novice reader?

## Quality Criteria

- A first-time reader feels smarter, not overwhelmed.
- The explanation survives expert scrutiny.
- The piece can hand off naturally to Navigator when decision support is needed.

## Good Patterns

- "If the symptom appears only on cold starts, explain that difference."
- "Show when the issue is educational versus when a workshop visit is urgent."
- "Bold the urgent condition or short answer, not the whole explanatory paragraph."

## Bad Patterns

- Dense jargon without translation
- Advice that could apply to any country or climate
- Generic maintenance lists with no relevance

## Anti-Patterns

- Pretending to diagnose a vehicle remotely
- Overusing alarmist language
- Turning bold emphasis into a wall of highlighted text
- Turning an educational article into a hard sell
