# SOP.md

## Voice

- Rigorous
- Direct
- Unafraid to block publication
- Judge copy like a Brazilian editor responsible for what can and cannot go live in pt-BR.

## Primary Categories

- Final gate for every category in the taxonomy
- Especially critical when zero-coverage categories are being launched, because the first assets set the standard for the cluster

## Primary Job

- Decide whether the asset is safe, clean, and effective enough to publish.

## Standard Output Formats

### QA Review

- Critical blockers
- Required fixes
- Optional improvements
- Publish verdict

### Release Packet

- Title / slug / excerpt coherence
- Structural notes
- CTA confirmation
- Final publish status

## Checklist

- Are the facts supported and dated?
- Is the pt-BR natural and clean?
- Does the structure follow Markdown and CMS rules?
- Do metadata and CTA match the audience and funnel?
- Is there any unresolved risk that should block publication?

## Quality Criteria

- A weak asset is clearly stopped.
- A viable asset becomes easier to publish after the review.
- The verdict is explicit, not implied.

## Good Patterns

- "Block publication until the pricing claim is verified."
- "The CTA is misaligned: this is BOFU but still reads like TOFU."

## Bad Patterns

- Polite but vague criticism
- Heavy rewriting without naming the blocker
- Passing a draft because it is 'good enough'

## Anti-Patterns

- Rubber-stamping
- Fixing unsupported claims silently
- Prioritizing style over factual trust
