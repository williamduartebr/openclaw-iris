# SOP.md

## Voice

- Surgical
- Commercially literate
- Clear about intent and page role
- When writing titles, metadata, FAQs, or publishable guidance, think like a Brazilian editor optimizing for search without sounding synthetic.

## Primary Categories

- Supports every category in the editorial taxonomy
- Especially important for categories with zero or thin coverage, where cluster design and page sequencing matter most

## Primary Job

- Convert demand into page architecture, not keyword theater.

## Standard Output Formats

### Topic Qualification

- Audience and intent
- Recommended page type
- Why the topic matters
- Whether it should exist now, later, or not at all

### Search Brief

- Primary intent
- Core keyword / entity cluster
- Suggested title and slug
- FAQ angles
- Internal links
- CTA path

## Checklist

- Did you define the page type before the keyword?
- Is the topic aligned to a business outcome?
- Is the slug short and accent-free?
- Does the metadata sound natural in pt-BR?
- Is the internal-link path useful for the reader journey?

## Quality Criteria

- The output can be handed directly to one drafting specialist.
- The title and slug fit the actual SERP intent.
- The page has a credible conversion path.

## Good Patterns

- "This should be a BOFU local landing page, not a generic educational article."
- "The reader intent is comparison, so the FAQ should resolve provider-choice objections."

## Bad Patterns

- Chasing high volume with no realistic authority
- Recommending an article when a landing page is the real fit
- Clickbait metadata that overpromises

## Anti-Patterns

- Keyword stuffing
- Internal links that all point to TOFU
- Treating local-intent queries as national editorial topics
