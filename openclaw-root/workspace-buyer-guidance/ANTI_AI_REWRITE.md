# ANTI_AI_REWRITE.md

Use this mode when William wants an article rewritten so it performs in 2026 search and cannot be replaced by a thin AI summary.

## Mission

Rewrite the article into something readers still want to click because it helps them decide, avoid mistakes, or understand the real cost of getting the decision wrong.

## Core Outcome

The finished article must improve:

- retention
- click motivation
- real-world usefulness
- decision confidence

## Critical Rules

- Do not sound like Wikipedia.
- Do not preserve filler just because the source article already had it.
- Do not spend the first paragraphs defining obvious terms.
- Do not stay neutral if the reader needs a recommendation.
- Do not hide uncertainty around cost, timing, urgency, or quality.

## Always Add

- wallet impact
- practical scenarios
- common mistakes
- consequences
- clear technical opinion
- a real tradeoff or difficult choice

## Cost Layer Rule

- If the article touches service, repair, parts, documentation, purchase risk, or provider choice, it should carry explicit wallet logic.
- Exact national pricing is optional when instability is high, but the reader should still see bounded BRL ranges, order-of-magnitude guidance, cost drivers, or the price of getting the decision wrong.
- A rewrite that talks about risk but never shows what can get expensive is still underpowered.

## Required Structure For Full Rewrites

1. Rewritten title
   Make it sharper, more clickable, and tied to cost, consequence, mistake, or risk.
2. Intro
   Start with a practical problem or tension, not a definition.
3. `O QUE MUDA NA PRATICA`
   Explain what changes in daily use, maintenance, cost, or decision pressure.
4. `QUANTO ISSO CUSTA (NA VIDA REAL)`
   Use bounded values, realistic BRL ranges, and comparison of cheap versus expensive choices.
5. `ERROS MAIS COMUNS`
   Show the mistake and the consequence of that mistake.
6. `ONDE ISSO PEGA DE VERDADE`
   Show where the issue becomes costly, urgent, or decision-relevant.
7. `VALE A PENA OU NAO?`
   Take a clear position. Explain when yes, when no, and what changes the answer.
8. Conclusion
   Reinforce the practical impact and next decision.

## Anti-AI Layer

Every rewritten article must include at least two of these:

- concrete examples
- real numbers or bounded ranges
- scenario comparisons
- error situations
- experience-led language such as `na pratica` or `o que acontece e`

## Self-Check Before Finalizing

Ask:

`Would this still be worth clicking if Google already answered the basic definition?`

If no, rewrite again until the article depends on judgment, consequence, cost, and lived reality rather than summary.
