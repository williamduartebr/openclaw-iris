# ANTI_AI_REWRITE.md

## Purpose

Use this playbook when William asks to rewrite, improve, or upgrade an existing automotive article that is factually acceptable but generic, easily summarized, or too weak for the AI Overview era.

The goal is not to "make it longer." The goal is to make it materially harder to replace with a Google summary by increasing decision value, practical tension, and click incentive.

## Routing Rule

- `seo-strategy` / Vector sharpens angle, title logic, CTR motive, and SERP differentiation.
- `buyer-guidance` / Navigator owns the body copy whenever cost, tradeoff, timing, risk, or "worth it or not" logic is central.
- `consumer-education` / Atlas should only own the body if the topic is still primarily explanatory after the rewrite brief.
- `quality-desk` / Sentinel must verify that the final piece is not generic, not institutional, and not easily answerable by a short AI summary.

## Non-Negotiable Rewrite Standards

- Do not rewrite in encyclopedia mode.
- Do not preserve generic definitions just because they were already in the article.
- Do not repeat obvious information the reader already expects.
- Do not confuse correctness with usefulness.
- Do not write a "safe" article that avoids taking a position.

## What The Upgraded Piece Must Add

- Real impact on the reader's wallet
- Practical scenarios and everyday use context
- Common user mistakes
- Consequences of the wrong choice or delayed action
- Clear technical opinion
- A real decision, conflict, or tradeoff

## Cost Layer Rule

- If the topic can plausibly affect the reader's wallet, the rewrite should not leave price logic implicit.
- Exact national pricing is not mandatory when the market is unstable, but the article should still include at least one of the following:
  - bounded BRL ranges
  - order-of-magnitude guidance
  - what usually makes the problem cheaper or more expensive
  - when paying more is justified
  - how much the wrong diagnosis, delay, or shortcut can cost
- "No exact price available" is not a license to publish a financially empty article.

## Mandatory Output Blocks

Use these exact pt-BR block ideas in the publishable article whenever the task is a full rewrite:

1. Rewritten title
2. Intro with a practical problem, tension, or consequence
3. `O QUE MUDA NA PRATICA`
4. `QUANTO ISSO CUSTA (NA VIDA REAL)`
5. `ERROS MAIS COMUNS`
6. `ONDE ISSO PEGA DE VERDADE`
7. `VALE A PENA OU NAO?`
8. Conclusion with practical impact

## Anti-AI Layer

Every rewrite must include at least two of the following:

- concrete examples
- real or bounded numbers
- scenario comparison
- user-error situations
- experiential phrasing such as `na pratica`, `o que acontece e`, `no bolso`, `quando isso da errado`

## Final Editorial Test

Before closing the task, ask:

`If Google showed the basic answer above the fold, would this article still earn the click?`

If the answer is no, the rewrite is still too generic.
