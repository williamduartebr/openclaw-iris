# MV Article Refresh B2B

Rewrite old B2B articles so they feel sharper, more commercial, and more useful to automotive business owners.

## Default outcome

Turn a weak legacy article into a stronger B2B asset that:

- captures a real owner pain
- connects visibility to contacts, contacts to revenue, and revenue to action
- sounds current and editorial, not institutional
- survives AI summaries by adding consequence, decision value, and practical friction
- keeps the same slug unless explicitly asked otherwise

## Preferred reframing

Usually reframe around one or more of these:

- the business is losing contacts before the first conversation
- the business appears weaker than it really is
- the owner confuses digital movement with result
- the operation depends too much on indication or old clients
- the company does not look easy to trust, compare, or contact
- the owner is paying hidden commercial cost for weak discovery

## Workflow

1. Fetch live page and CMS record.
2. Preserve slug/URL by default.
3. Keep or swap cover according to the user request.
4. Rewrite intro around a concrete business loss, friction, or hidden commercial consequence.
5. Replace generic sections with decision-driven sections.
6. Upgrade title, excerpt, and SEO description.
7. Remove weak inline images if needed.
8. PATCH article with latest version.
9. Report what changed and list any old images safe to delete.

## Writing rules

- Do not open with category-level generalizations like `Muita empresa...`.
- Do not repeat the same broad opener pattern across adjacent articles (`muita empresa`, `muita oficina`, `muito negócio`, etc.). Rotate lead structures deliberately.
- Do not write as a generic marketing explainer.
- Do not confuse awareness with traffic or traffic with result.
- Do not use all-caps H2s by default.
- Do not let excerpt/SEO openings repeat the same cluster formula.
- Use variation on purpose: alternate between consequence-first, trust-first, search-behavior-first, friction-first, and hidden-loss-first openings so the cluster does not sound templated.
- Prefer operational language: agenda, orçamento, demanda, confiança, contato, indicação, margem, previsibilidade.

## Preferred section types

Use headings such as:

- where the business is losing demand without noticing
- what the owner is reading wrong in digital performance
- why the customer leaves before the first contact
- what weak visibility costs in the real business
- what to fix first without turning it into a giant project

## Final report

Tell the user:

- article id
- whether slug stayed the same
- whether the cover stayed or changed
- current version
- what changed editorially
- which old image path(s)/URL(s) can be deleted
