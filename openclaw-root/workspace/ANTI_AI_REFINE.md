# ANTI_AI_REFINE.md

## Purpose

Use this playbook when William provides an article that is already SEO-optimized and asks for refinement rather than a full rewrite.

This mode is for strengthening the article against AI Overviews without destroying the current structure or replacing the draft wholesale.

## Core Rule

Refine, do not restart.

Keep the existing structure unless there is a clear structural weakness that prevents comprehension, retention, or click value.

## What Refinement Must Add

- real or bounded BRL values
- at least one practical scenario
- stronger consequence framing
- clearer decision contrast
- at least one strong insight line
- a more impactful opening if the intro is still weak
- cost logic even when exact national pricing is too unstable to state cleanly

## What Refinement Must Not Do

- Do not rewrite the article from zero.
- Do not remove useful sections only to impose a new template.
- Do not flatten the article into generic explanation.
- Do not simplify the text until it loses authority or practical weight.

## Mandatory Upgrade Moves

### 1. Insert real-world cost

Whenever plausible, add:

- part cost
- labor or diagnosis range
- financial impact of the wrong decision
- what usually pushes the case toward the cheaper or more expensive end
- when a higher upfront spend avoids a larger downstream loss

Example logic:

- `isso pode custar entre R$ 150 e R$ 800 dependendo do caso`
- `o valor sobe quando entra troca de componente, retrabalho ou diagnostico por tentativa`

### 2. Insert at least one real scenario

Use practical phrasing such as:

- `um caso comum e`
- `na oficina, isso aparece quando`
- `na pratica, muita gente faz o caminho errado`

### 3. Create decision contrast

Make the reader compare:

- cheap now versus expensive later
- diagnose right versus trocar por tentativa
- preventive action versus ignoring the symptom

### 4. Reinforce consequences

When an error is mentioned, explain:

- what happens if the reader ignores it
- how the issue becomes more expensive
- how a bad diagnosis or wrong repair escalates the problem

### 5. Strengthen the intro if needed

If the current opening is bland, inject:

- risk
- cost
- consequence

without replacing the entire article body.

### 7. Break repeated metadata templates

If the article belongs to a cluster or recent run of publications:

- vary title, subtitle, excerpt, and SEO-description openings
- avoid defaulting again to `Veja o que...`, `Veja por que...`, `Descubra por que...`, or `Entenda como...`
- make the metadata feel editorially distinct enough that adjacent pages do not read like copies with nouns swapped

### 8. Add one hard-hitting insight

Examples of the right energy:

- `o problema mais caro nao e o defeito, e o diagnostico errado`
- `na pratica, o prejuizo vem da tentativa e erro`

## Routing Rule

- `seo-strategy` / Vector protects the click motive and refresh angle.
- `buyer-guidance` / Navigator owns the body when cost, tradeoff, mistake, or decision weight is central.
- `consumer-education` / Atlas can own the body only if the piece remains primarily educational after refinement.
- `quality-desk` / Sentinel checks that the article gained practical weight without losing structural coherence.

## Final Editorial Test

Before closing, ask:

`Does this refined version now contain details, numbers, or consequences that an AI summary would not deliver easily?`

If no, add more real scenarios, financial ranges, or diagnostic consequences.
